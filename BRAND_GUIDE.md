# Ocean Grown Seeds: Brand & Style Guide

## 1. Brand Identity & Positioning
- **Industry:** Cannabis Genetics & Seed Bank (Artisan/Small Batch)
- **Tagline:** "Start a New Life" / "Highly Hybridized Cannabis Genetics"
- **Heritage:** Founded around 2008, lead breeder Vader. Legacy roots, artisan craftsmanship, and a community-first approach. Known for strains like *Jawa Pie*, *Alien Rift*, and *Vader OG*.
- **Vibe / Archetype:** The "Modern Genetics Lab" meets "Natural Elements". It needs to give off a highly clean, research-driven, and laboratory vibe while staying true to its organic, ocean-grown roots. Clinical precision mixed with organic fluidity.

## 2. Color Palette
*Shifting from a heavy dark mode to a clean, bright, laboratory-inspired palette with stark accents.*
- **Primary Backgrounds:** Clinical White / Frost / Very Light Pearl (Creates the pristine "clean room" or laboratory aesthetic).
- **Primary Brand Color:** Deep Ocean/Forest Green (Used for stark contrast against the white backgrounds, grounding the brand in nature).
- **Secondary / Text:** Deep Charcoal / Ink Black (Softer than pure black for high-end readability on light backgrounds).
- **Accent 1 (Modernization/Tech):** Electric Cyan or Bright Aqua (Represents the "Ocean" element, hybridization, and tech/modern appeal).
- **Accent 2:** Subtle Silver / Lab Gray (For borders, structural UI elements, and topographic lines).

## 3. Typography
- **Headings (The "Bold Legacy"):** A modern, clean sans-serif with geometric precision (e.g., *Montserrat*, *Inter*, or *Helvetica Now*). Clean and authoritative.
- **Body (The "Accessible Guide"):** A highly legible sans-serif for detailed reading.
- **Accents (The "Lab Data"):** A strict monospaced font (e.g., *Fira Code*, *Space Mono*, or *JetBrains Mono*). This is crucial for the "Research/Lab" vibe—used for genetic lineages, THC percentages, flowering times, and batch numbers.

## 4. Visual Language & Imagery
- **Photography:** Clean, bright, high-resolution macro photography of seeds, trichomes, and plants, ideally on clean white or stark black backgrounds to make the specimen the sole focus (like scientific documentation).
- **Graphic Elements:** 
  - **Fluid Wave Vectors & Topography Lines:** Used as subtle, sophisticated background patterns in the light gray/silver accent color to break up the white space without cluttering it. Blends the concept of earth/water with scientific mapping.
  - Minimalist, thin-line iconography for strain stats (yield, flowering time, indica/sativa ratio).

## 5. UI/UX Direction
- **Clean & Clinical UI:** Lots of negative (white) space. Structural layouts that feel like a well-organized research dossier or genetic database.
- **Strain Cards (The "Specimen Files"):** Clean white cards with subtle drop shadows or thin silver borders. They should look like modern digital lab readouts, using the monospace font heavily for the genetic data.
- **Navigation:** Highly structured, intuitive, and distraction-free.

## 6. Tone of Voice
- **Authoritative, Scientific, yet Approachable:** They have 20+ years of experience. The copy should sound like an expert botanist or lead researcher presenting their finest work.
- **Craft-Focused:** Emphasize words like "Artisan," "Small Batch," "Highly Hybridized," "Precision," and "Optimal Results."
